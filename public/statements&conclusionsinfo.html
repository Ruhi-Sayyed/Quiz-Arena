What Are Statements and Conclusions?

This topic tests your ability to evaluate whether conclusions logically follow from given statements. It helps improve critical thinking and comprehension.

How to Approach These Questions

  • Understand the Statement: Carefully read and analyze what is explicitly stated.
  • Check the Conclusion: Determine if the conclusion necessarily follows from the statement without assumptions.
  • Types of Valid Conclusions: Conclusions can be definitely true, possibly true, or definitely false based on the statement.
  • Avoid Assumptions: Do not use outside knowledge, only the information given.

Example Question

Statement: All cats are animals.

Conclusion 1: Some animals are cats.

Conclusion 2: All animals are cats.

Answer: Conclusion 1 logically follows (some animals are cats), but conclusion 2 does not.

Practice Example

Statement: No dogs are cats.
Conclusion 1: Some dogs are not cats.
Conclusion 2: Some cats are dogs.

Answer: Conclusion 1 follows logically, Conclusion 2 does not.
