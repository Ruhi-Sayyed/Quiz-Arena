Learn Simplification

Quick and Accurate Calculation Tips

What is Simplification?

Simplification means reducing complex mathematical expressions into simpler or easier forms to calculate mentally or on paper quickly.

It helps save time in competitive exams by avoiding lengthy calculations and focusing on shortcuts and smart approaches.

Basic Rules & Tips

  • Use BODMAS/BIDMAS rule: Brackets, Orders (powers and roots), Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction.
  • Perform operations inside brackets first.
  • Handle powers and roots before multiplication and division.
  • Perform multiplication and division from left to right.
  • Perform addition and subtraction last, from left to right.
  • Use approximation for quick estimation.

Examples

Example 1:

(8 + 4) × 3 - 12 ÷ 4

Solution:

  • First solve inside brackets: (8 + 4) = 12
  • Then multiplication and division left to right: 12 × 3 = 36; 12 ÷ 4 = 3
  • Finally subtraction: 36 - 3 = 33

Example 2:

16 ÷ 4 + 2 × (6 - 3)

Solution:

  • Brackets first: (6 - 3) = 3
  • Division and multiplication from left to right: 16 ÷ 4 = 4; 2 × 3 = 6
  • Addition: 4 + 6 = 10

Example 3:

5 + 2 × 32 - 8 ÷ 4

Solution:

  • Power first: 32 = 9
  • Multiplication and division from left to right: 2 × 9 = 18; 8 ÷ 4 = 2
  • Addition and subtraction: 5 + 18 - 2 = 21

Example 4:

(12 + 8) ÷ 5 + 6 × 2

Solution:

  • Brackets: 12 + 8 = 20
  • Division and multiplication: 20 ÷ 5 = 4; 6 × 2 = 12
  • Addition: 4 + 12 = 16
