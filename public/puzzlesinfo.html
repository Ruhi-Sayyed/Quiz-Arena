What Are Logic Puzzles?

Logic puzzles require reasoning and deduction to find solutions. They improve problem-solving skills by challenging you to think systematically.

Types of Logic Puzzles

  • Sudoku: Fill a 9×9 grid so each row, column, and 3×3 box contains digits 1 through 9 without repeats.
    Example: In a mini 4×4 Sudoku, fill the grid so every row, column, and 2×2 box contains numbers 1 through 4 without repeats.
  • Grid Puzzles: Use clues to match people, places, or objects in a logic grid.
    Example: Given clues about three friends’ favorite fruits and cities, determine who likes what and where they live.
  • Direction Puzzles: Solve problems involving movements or directions to find locations or sequences.
    Example: “John walks 3 blocks north, then 2 blocks east. Where is he relative to his starting point?”
  • Filler Puzzles: Arrange shapes or blocks to fit into a defined space perfectly.
    Example: Fit given Tetris-like shapes into a rectangular grid without overlapping or gaps.

How to Solve Them

  • Start with Certainties: Identify definite information from the clues before guessing.
  • Use Elimination: Rule out impossible options to narrow down possibilities.
  • Keep Notes: Mark deductions clearly to avoid confusion in complex puzzles.

Example Grid Puzzle

Consider the following puzzle where you match names, pets, and cities:

Name     | Pet    | City
-------- | ------ | -------
Alice    | Dog    | Tokyo
Bob      | Cat    | Paris
Clara    | Fish   | London

Clues:
- The person who owns the cat lives in Paris.
- Alice doesn’t live in London.
- Clara does not own the dog.

Solution:

  • Since the cat owner lives in Paris, Bob must own the cat and live in Paris.
  • Alice doesn’t live in London, so she lives in Tokyo with the dog.
  • Clara owns the fish and lives in London.
