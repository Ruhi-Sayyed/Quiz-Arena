Understanding Percentages Made Easy

What is Percentage?

A percentage represents a fraction out of 100. It is used to express how much part of a whole something is.

Percentages are widely used in discounts, interest rates, statistics, and many real-life calculations.

Key Formulas & Concepts

  • Percentage (%) = (Part / Whole) × 100
  • To find part when percentage and whole are known: Part = (Percentage × Whole) / 100
  • To find whole when percentage and part are known: Whole = (Part × 100) / Percentage
  • Percentage increase/decrease = (Change / Original) × 100

Examples

Example 1:

What is 20% of 150?

Solution:

  • Use formula: (20 × 150) ÷ 100 = 3000 ÷ 100 = 30

Example 2:

If a product originally costs $500 and is sold at $400, what is the percentage discount?

Solution:

  • Change = 500 - 400 = 100
  • Percentage discount = (100 / 500) × 100 = 20%

Example 3:

What is the whole if 25 is 10% of it?

Solution:

  • Whole = (25 × 100) ÷ 10 = 250

Example 4:

A population increased from 1200 to 1500. Find the percentage increase.

Solution:

  • Change = 1500 - 1200 = 300
  • Percentage increase = (300 / 1200) × 100 = 25%
