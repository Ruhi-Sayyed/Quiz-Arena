Calculating Interest Made Easy

What is Simple Interest?

Simple Interest (SI) is the interest calculated only on the principal amount, or the original sum of money deposited or lent.

It does not compound, meaning interest is not calculated on previously earned interest.

Key Formula

  • SI = (P × R × T) / 100, where:
    • P = Principal amount
    • R = Rate of interest per year (%)
    • T = Time period in years

Examples

Example 1:

Find the simple interest on $5,000 at 6% per annum for 3 years.

Solution:

  • SI = (5000 × 6 × 3) / 100 = 900

Example 2:

Calculate the principal if the interest earned is $2400 at 8% per annum for 4 years.

Solution:

  • 2400 = (P × 8 × 4) / 100
  • P = (2400 × 100) / (8 × 4) = 7500

Example 3:

Find the rate of interest if $1500 interest is earned on $10,000 for 2 years.

Solution:

  • 1500 = (10000 × R × 2) / 100
  • R = (1500 × 100) / (10000 × 2) = 7.5%

Example 4:

Calculate the time period if simple interest is $720 on $6000 at 6% per annum.

Solution:

  • 720 = (6000 × 6 × T) / 100
  • T = (720 × 100) / (6000 × 6) = 2 years
