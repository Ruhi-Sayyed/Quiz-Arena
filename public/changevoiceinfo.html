Change of Voice (Active & Passive)

Master converting sentences between active and passive voice easily.

Understanding Voice

Voice indicates whether the subject of a sentence is performing the action (Active Voice) or receiving the action (Passive Voice).

How to Convert Active to Passive

  • Identify the object of the active sentence (this becomes the subject in passive).
  • Use the correct form of the verb "to be" + past participle of the main verb.
  • Optionally add the doer of the action using by + agent.

Important Notes

  • Passive voice is commonly used when the doer is unknown or unimportant.
  • Not all active sentences can be passivized (e.g., intransitive verbs).
  • The tense of the passive verb must match the active verb tense.

Examples

Active: The chef cooks the meal.
Passive: The meal is cooked by the chef.

Active: She wrote a letter.
Passive: A letter was written by her.

Active: They are cleaning the house.
Passive: The house is being cleaned by them.

Active: Someone has stolen my wallet.
Passive: My wallet has been stolen.

Active: The teacher will explain the lesson.
Passive: The lesson will be explained by the teacher.

Try to Convert

  • Active: The dog bit the man.
  • Active: They will complete the project soon.
  • Active: The kids are watching a movie.
  • Active: She had finished the report before the meeting.
