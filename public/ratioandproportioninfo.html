Learn Ratio and Proportion

Understanding the Relationship Between Quantities

What are Ratio and Proportion?

A Ratio compares two quantities showing how many times one value contains or is contained within the other.

A Proportion states that two ratios are equal or equivalent.

These concepts are very useful in solving real-life problems like mixing ingredients, sharing money, and scaling.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Ratio of A to B is written as A : B or A/B.
  • Proportion: A : B = C : D means A × D = B × C (cross multiplication).
  • To find a missing term in proportion, use cross multiplication.
  • Ratios can be simplified by dividing both terms by their greatest common divisor (GCD).

Examples

Example 1:

Ratio of boys to girls is 3:4. If there are 12 boys, how many girls?

Solution:

  • 3 : 4 = 12 : x
  • Cross multiply: 3 × x = 4 × 12
  • x = 48 / 3 = 16 girls

Example 2:

Solve for x: 5 : x = 15 : 9

Solution:

  • Cross multiply: 5 × 9 = 15 × x
  • 45 = 15x ⇒ x = 45 / 15 = 3

Example 3:

Simplify the ratio 18 : 24

Solution:

  • GCD of 18 and 24 is 6
  • Divide both by 6: 18 ÷ 6 : 24 ÷ 6 = 3 : 4

Example 4:

Check if ratios 4 : 6 and 8 : 12 are in proportion

Solution:

  • Cross multiply: 4 × 12 = 48, 6 × 8 = 48
  • Since both are equal, ratios are in proportion
